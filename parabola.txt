# Lesson Plan: Parabolas

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

* Explain what a parabola is geometrically.
* Identify the focus, directrix, and vertex of a parabola.
* Understand the meaning of the parameter p.
* Relate the graph of a parabola to its equation.
* Predict how moving the focus or directrix changes the parabola's shape.
* Create parabolas with specified properties.

---

## Page 1 — Meet the Parabola

### Type

Explore

### Goal

Introduce the geometric definition of a parabola.

### Prompt

A parabola is the set of all points that are the same distance from a focus and a directrix.

### Interactive Elements

* Coordinate plane
* Focus point (F)
* Directrix line
* Parabola drawn automatically

### User Actions

* Drag the focus up and down.
* Drag the directrix up and down.
* Watch the parabola update in real time.

### Visual Features

* Highlight a point on the parabola.
* Draw:

  * Distance from the point to the focus
  * Distance from the point to the directrix

Show that the two distances are equal.

### Success Condition

Click "Continue."

---

## Page 2 — Explore Freely

### Type

Explore

### Goal

Build intuition for how the focus and directrix determine the parabola.

### Prompt

Move the focus and directrix around.

What changes?
What stays the same?

### Interactions

User can:

* Drag focus vertically
* Drag directrix vertically
* Toggle labels:

  * Focus
  * Directrix
  * Vertex

### Hints

* What happens when the focus moves farther away?
* What happens when the focus gets closer to the directrix?
* Can you make the parabola wider?
* Can you make it narrower?

### Success Condition

Move both the focus and directrix at least once.

---

## Page 3 — Discover the Vertex

### Type

Explore

### Goal

Understand where the vertex comes from.

### Prompt

Notice the lowest point of the parabola.

This point is called the vertex.

### Interactive Elements

* Vertex highlighted automatically

### Explanation

The vertex is exactly halfway between:

* The focus
* The directrix

### Challenge

Move the focus.

Predict where the vertex will move.

### Success Condition

Correctly answer:

"Where is the vertex located?"

A. At the focus

B. Halfway between the focus and directrix

C. On the directrix

Correct Answer: B

---

## Page 4 — Introducing p

### Type

Explore

### Goal

Introduce the parameter p.

### Prompt

The distance from the vertex to the focus is called p.

### Visual Features

Display:

p = distance from vertex to focus

### Interactions

User drags the focus.

Value of p updates dynamically.

### Observation Questions

* What happens when p gets larger?
* What happens when p gets smaller?

### Key Takeaway

Larger p → wider parabola

Smaller p → narrower parabola

### Success Condition

Adjust p to at least three different values.

---

## Page 5 — The Equation Appears

### Type

Explore

### Goal

Connect the geometry to the algebra.

### Equation Display

For a vertical parabola:

x² = 4py

### Interactive Elements

As the user moves the focus:

* p updates
* Equation updates automatically

Examples:

p = 1

x² = 4y

p = 2

x² = 8y

p = 3

x² = 12y

### Prompt

What number changes when p changes?

### Success Condition

Answer:

"The coefficient depends on ____."

Correct Answer: p

---

## Page 6 — Move the Vertex

### Type

Explore

### Goal

Introduce vertex form.

### Equation Display

(x − h)² = 4p(y − k)

### Explanation

(h, k) is the vertex.

### Interactive Elements

User can drag the entire parabola.

As they drag:

* Vertex moves
* Equation updates live

Example:

Vertex = (3, 2)

(x − 3)² = 4p(y − 2)

### Observation

h controls left/right position.

k controls up/down position.

### Success Condition

Move the vertex to a target location.

Example:

Move the vertex to (2, 1).

---

## Page 7 — Prediction Challenge

### Type

Challenge

### Goal

Connect graph features to equation components.

### Prompt

Suppose:

* Vertex = (0, 0)
* Focus = (0, 3)

Predict:

1. What is p?
2. What is the equation?

### Expected Answer

p = 3

x² = 12y

### Feedback

Correct:
Great! You connected the geometry to the equation.

Incorrect:
Remember that p is the distance from the vertex to the focus.

## Page 8 — Create a Specific Parabola

### Type

Challenge

### Goal

Apply understanding independently.

### Prompt

Create a parabola with:

* Vertex at (0,0)
* Focus at (0,2)

### Validation

Check:

* Focus position
* Directrix position
* Resulting equation

### Feedback

Correct:
Nice work! You successfully constructed the parabola.

Hint:
The directrix should be the same distance below the vertex as the focus is above it.

---

## Page 9 — Reflection

### Type

Reflection

### Question

Which statement is true?

A. A parabola is determined only by its focus.

B. A parabola is determined only by its directrix.

C. A parabola is determined by both the focus and directrix.

D. A parabola is determined by its vertex alone.

### Correct Answer

C

### Feedback

The focus and directrix together define every point on the parabola.
